Famous Landmarks in Nepal

Explore the rich heritage and breathtaking landmarks of Nepal

Pashupatinath Temple

Pashupatinath Temple is one of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. Situated on the banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Pashupatinath Temple
Pashupatinath Temple is not only a place of worship but also an architectural marvel with intricate wood and stone carvings. Every year, thousands of devotees visit during the festival of Maha Shivaratri.

Lumbini

Lumbini is the birthplace of Lord Buddha, making it a revered pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide. It features peaceful gardens and historical monuments.

Lumbini
Lumbini is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that houses the Maya Devi Temple, marking the exact spot where Buddha was born. It is surrounded by monasteries built by different Buddhist countries.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a historic center renowned for its stunning palaces, temples, and traditional Newari architecture.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square
Bhaktapur Durbar Square is famous for the 55-Window Palace and the Nyatapola Temple, a five-tiered pagoda that is an architectural masterpiece.

Sagarmatha (Mount Everest)

Sagarmatha, known internationally as Mount Everest, is the highest peak in the world and a major draw for climbers and adventurers.

Sagarmatha (Mount Everest)
Mount Everest, located in the Sagarmatha National Park, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unique biodiversity, including rare species like the snow leopard.
